# International & domestic-market GEO (especially the Chinese market)

The rest of this skill assumes the **Western AI answer stack** (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude, Copilot) and a *"your own site + earned third-party citations"* model. For a brand targeting **China / Chinese-speaking users**, both the engines **and** the leverage hierarchy genuinely change. Apply this overlay whenever the audience is mainland-Chinese, or the brand is bilingual.

> The core thesis still holds — *off-site citation footprint is the dominant lever, on-site only makes you eligible* — but **where the citations live moves from the open web to big content platforms.**

## 1. The engines are different (Phase 0)

Your target-engine list (Phase 0) must include the domestic engines when relevant — don't assume ChatGPT/Perplexity even reach the audience (many are access-limited in mainland China):

- **豆包 Doubao** (ByteDance) · **Kimi** (Moonshot) · **DeepSeek** · **文心一言 Ernie** (Baidu) · **通义千问 Qwen** (Alibaba) · **腾讯元宝 Yuanbao** · **Baidu AI search**.

Measurement (Phase 4) must **prompt-test these domestic engines**, not just the Western five — same method (fixed prompt set, ~3 runs/engine/month, track the trend), different engines.

## 2. The leverage hierarchy inverts: platform content > your own site

In the West you optimize your own site and earn citations across the open web. In China, **the center of gravity shifts to the big content platforms** that both users and the domestic engines trust and index:

- **小红书 (Xiaohongshu / RedNote)** · **知乎 (Zhihu)** · **B站 (Bilibili)** · **微信公众号 (WeChat Official Accounts)** · **抖音 (Douyin)** · 百家号 / 头条号 / 微博.
- Domestic engines lean heavily on their **parent ecosystem's** content: 豆包 ↔ 抖音/今日头条; 文心 ↔ 百度百科/百度知道; 腾讯元宝 ↔ 微信公众号/搜狗. **Being the cited answer *on* these platforms usually beats anything on your own domain.**

So the **off-site program (Phase 3) re-weights** for China: instead of Reddit / HARO / Wikidata, you produce genuinely useful **platform content** — a 知乎 answer, a 小红书 笔记, a 公众号 文章, a B站 视频 — that the domestic engines retrieve and quote. Same rules as the Reddit playbook (value-first, answer in the post itself, no spam), different venues.

## 3. On-site still matters — but lead with it even less

On-site eligibility (crawlable text, schema, answer-first structure) still helps Baidu's index and any engine that fetches your site. Keep doing it — but for a purely domestic audience it moves the needle even *less* than in the West, because the engines retrieve from platforms first. **Don't skip it; don't lead with it.**

## 4. Entity anchors have domestic equivalents

| Western (rest of skill) | Domestic equivalent |
| --- | --- |
| Wikidata / Wikipedia | **百度百科 (Baidu Baike)** — the one the engines read most; 搜狗百科, 互动百科 |
| Google Business Profile | **高德地图 / 百度地图** business listing (local) |
| "Best of" listicles | platform 榜单 / 合集 (知乎 / 小红书 collections) |
| Editorial backlinks | **平台权重 + 跨平台被提及** — being referenced across 知乎/小红书/公众号 is the citation footprint |

## 5. Bilingual brands run two programs in parallel

A bilingual brand (EN + 中文) effectively runs **two GEO programs at once**: the Western stack on the own-site/earned-citation model, and the domestic stack on the platform-content model — **separate target-prompt cohorts, separate measurement**. They rarely share leverage, so don't try to make one program serve both.

## 6. Red lines still apply

No bought traffic, fake engagement, or coordinated 刷量 — domestic platforms **and** the engines that read them penalize it, just like Google penalizes link schemes. Original, specific, genuinely useful is still the standard.

## 7. Listening / baseline

Use the operator's **`agent-reach` skill** (if available) as the listening arm for domestic platforms (小红书 / 知乎 / B站 / 微博) — to find where the target questions are actually asked and who currently owns the answer. That's the domestic equivalent of the Phase-0 baseline.
