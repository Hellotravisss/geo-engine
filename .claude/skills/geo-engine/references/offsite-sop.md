# Phase 3 — Off-site citation building (the real lever)

This is where ~80% of the GEO result is won, and where most competitors won't do the work — your edge. The mechanism: AI recommends entities that appear, with consistent framing, across **many trusted third-party places**. That feeds both the retrieval path (the engine searches and finds you cited) and the memory path (repeated mentions enter training data). It's the same underlying logic as backlinks — earned, not bought.

This is a **sustained program, not a one-off** — frame it to the client as the retainer. Run three fronts in parallel, ranked by ROI.

> The strongest off-site asset is **original data nobody else has** (Phase 2). Every front below works far better when you lead with a citable number instead of asking for a favor. Build the data asset first; it's the bait for all three fronts.

---

## Front 1 — Community (Reddit + niche forums) — highest ROI

Google and OpenAI have data deals with Reddit, and LLMs cite Reddit heavily. A genuinely helpful answer that happens to reference the brand's data is the single most cost-effective citation you can earn. But these communities are ruthless about self-promotion — break the rules and you lose the account and the brand's reputation.

### Hard rules (violate one and it backfires)
1. **Build credit first.** A new account answers only — no links — for the first 1–2 weeks. Strict subs (e.g. r/PersonalFinanceCanada) auto-remove new self-promoters.
2. **Answer-first, link-as-source.** The numbers/answer go in the comment body. The link is just "source/verify" — the answer must stand on its own without it.
3. **Frequency.** Link-bearing answers ≤ 1/day/subreddit; pure-text answers unlimited.
4. **Removed once → go text-only** in that sub (still brand-valuable), retry links in two weeks.
5. **Never start promo threads.** Only answer others' questions. A site link in your profile bio is fine.

### Daily 10-minute workflow
Keep a set of saved searches (sorted newest + relevant) for the subs where the target questions get asked. Pick 1–3 threads that have a real question and no good answer yet, and answer with the brand's real data.

### Answer template (generalize per niche)
> [Direct answer with the specific number first.] At $70,000 in Ontario you'd take home roughly **$53,600/yr (~$4,470/mo)** after federal + provincial tax, CPP and EI.
>
> [One line of useful context — the "why" or a comparison.]
>
> Source / check your exact figure: [link to the exact page that proves the number]

The pattern is archetype-independent: **lead with the number, add context, link as proof.** For a SaaS, the "number" might be a benchmark or a feature-comparison fact; for ecommerce, a real spec or price comparison. Use a **real human account with a real email** — the client's, not a sock puppet.

### Also worth it
- **Niche forums** with looser link rules than Reddit (e.g. RedFlagDeals for Canadian finance, indie-hacker forums for SaaS).
- **Quora** answers to questions in the niche.
- **Hacker News / Slack/Discord communities** for B2B/SaaS (same value-first rules).

## Front 2 — Original-data outreach (editorial citations) — highest quality

The goal is **real, editorial mentions that carry weight** — a journalist or respected blog quoting the brand's data and linking it. Ten genuinely relevant citations beat a hundred directory links. **Lead with data; never beg for a link.**

### ⛔ Never touch (these get domains penalized — worst during a new site's sandbox)
- Generic "write for us / submit guest post / paid inclusion" guest-post farms.
- Fiverr link sellers, PBNs, link-exchange groups, mass low-quality directory blasts.
These are link schemes Google explicitly targets.

### 2a. Journalists / press (free, top quality)
Register the founder on journalist-request platforms and respond — as the brand's expert — to relevant queries, attaching the brand's data article as the source. One mention in a real outlet beats 100 directory links.
- **Featured.com** (HARO's successor), **Qwoted**, **SourceBottle** (region-dependent).
- Respond fast, lead with a concrete data point, attach the citable URL.

### 2b. Resource-page & tool-list inclusion (scalable, mid-quality)
Ask relevant sites to add the brand to an existing "tools/resources" list — **not** a guest post, just a useful addition. Find their contact/about email, reference a specific article of theirs (proof you read it), and pitch the tool/data as useful to *their* readers.

### 2c. Listicle inclusion ("best [category]") — directly moves AI answers
"Best [category]" articles are exactly what LLMs read to answer "what's the best X". Find the ones ranking for the target queries, identify the author, and request inclusion — strongest when you can offer a reason they'd want you in (unique feature, original data, free tier). Getting into the listicles your competitors are in is often the fastest AI-visibility win.

### Pitch templates

**A — Data citation (to a blogger/journalist; strongest):**
> Subject: [Specific] data, free to cite
>
> Hi [Name], I run [Brand]. I just published original data on [topic] — e.g. [concrete, surprising stat with two numbers]. If it's useful for your readers it's free to cite or republish with a link: [URL]. Happy to pull a custom figure for [their angle] if helpful. — [Name]

**B — Resource/tool-list inclusion:**
> Subject: A free [tool] for your [readers/resource list]
>
> Hi [Name], loved your guide on [specific article]. For readers [doing the thing], I built [tool] — [one differentiator, e.g. free, no signup, covers X]. If you keep a tools/resources list it might be a useful add: [URL]. Either way, thanks for the great content. — [Name]

### Outreach rules
- **Personalize every email** — reference one specific piece of their work. Mass-merge templates are obvious and deleted.
- **Lead with value** (their readers benefit), not "please link me".
- **One at a time**, not blasts. **Follow up once** at day 7, polite close at day 21 — max two touches.
- **Real person, real email** (the client's). Log every send.

## Front 3 — Entity & authority signals

- **Wikidata item.** Easier to create than a Wikipedia article, and LLMs read Wikidata. Create an item for the brand/founder with the canonical entity sentence, official site, and `sameAs` links — a structured, authoritative anchor for the entity. (Wikipedia itself only if genuinely notable with independent coverage — don't force it.)
- **"Best of" lists & roundups** — see 2c; also a strong authority signal.
- **Quality directories & profiles** relevant to the niche (G2/Capterra for SaaS, Product Hunt for tools, industry associations for services) — selective, real ones only.
- **Google Business Profile** (local/service brands) — claim it; set category, service area, website, photos, services. Major for "[service] in [city]" and Maps. Seed early, genuine reviews. Keep NAP (name/address/phone) identical to the site and every directory.
- **Partner/client credit links** — designers, vendors, and clients you work with linking back to the same entity + URL.
